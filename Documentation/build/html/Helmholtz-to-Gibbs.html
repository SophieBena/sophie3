Derivative relations for a Helmholtz Model¶

This notebook provides a summary of conversion relations between derivatives of the Helmholtz free energy and teh Gibbs free energy, each expressed in terms of natural variables (T, V) and (T, P), respectively.

Autogenerated Helmholtz free energy derivatives:¶

\({\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_V}\) \({\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial V}}} \right)_T}\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_V}\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)_T}\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_V}\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial V}}} \right)\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right)\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {V^3}}}} \right)_T}\)

Identities that relate the Helmholtz and Gibbs free energies:¶

\(dA = - SdT - PdV = {\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_V}dT + {\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial V}}} \right)_T}dV\)
\(G = A + PV\)
\(dG = dA + PdV + VdP\)
\(dG = - SdT + VdP = {\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_V}dT + VdP\)

We seek definitions for the following derivatives of the Gibbs free energy:¶

\({\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\) \({\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T}\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P}\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right)\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {P^2}}}} \right)_T}\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_P}\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial P}}} \right)\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial T\partial {P^2}}}} \right)\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {P^3}}}} \right)_T}\)

(1) \({\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\)¶

\({\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} = {\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_V} = - S\)

(2) \({\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T}\)¶

\({\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_P} = V\)

Some definitions:¶

\(P = - {\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial V}}} \right)_T}\)
\(dP = - d{\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial V}}} \right)_T} = - \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)dT - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)_T}dV\)
\({\left( {\frac{{\partial P}}{{\partial V}}} \right)_T} = - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)_T}\) and \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {P^2}}}} \right)_T} = {\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T}\)
\(0 = - \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right) - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)_T}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\)
\({\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} = - \frac{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)}}{{{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)}_T}}}\) and \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right) = {\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\)

From:¶

\(d{\left( {\frac{{\partial G}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} = d{\left( {\frac{{\partial A}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_V} = {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_V}dT + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)dV\) and
\({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P} = {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_V} + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\) ### (3) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P}\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P} = {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_V} - \frac{{{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)}^2}}}{{{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)}_T}}}\)
#### Note: \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_V} = - {\left( {\frac{{\partial S}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_V} = - \frac{{{C_V}}}{T}\) and \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P} = - {\left( {\frac{{\partial S}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} = - \frac{{{C_P}}}{T}\)

(4) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right)\)¶

\(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right) = - \frac{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)}}{{{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)}_T}}}\)

(5) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {P^2}}}} \right)_T}\)¶

\({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {P^2}}}} \right)_T} = {\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T} = - \frac{1}{{{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)}_T}}}\)

Interlude …¶

\(d{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P} = d{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_V} + {\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}d\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right) + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)d{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\)
\(d{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P} = {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_V}dT + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial V}}} \right)dV + {\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\left[ {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial V}}} \right)dT + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right)dV} \right] + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)\left[ {{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)}_P}dT + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right)dP} \right]\) or ### (6) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_P}\) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_P} = {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_V} + 2\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial V}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P}\)
#### Note: \({\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\) is defined above in terms of Helmoltz derivatives. For the second derivative, \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P}\), the identity is:
\(0 = - \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial V}}} \right) - 2\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {V^3}}}} \right)_T}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)_T}{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial {T^2}}}} \right)_P}\)
For reference, derivatives of the heat capacity are obtained as:
\({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_P} = - {\left( {\frac{{\partial S}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P} = \frac{1}{T}\left[ {\frac{{{C_P}}}{T} - \left( {\frac{{\partial {C_P}}}{{\partial T}}} \right)} \right]\) and \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^3}}}} \right)_V} = \frac{1}{T}\left[ {\frac{{{C_V}}}{T} - {{\left( {\frac{{\partial {C_V}}}{{\partial T}}} \right)}_V}} \right]\) ### (7) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial P}}} \right)\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}G}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial P}}} \right) = \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {T^2}\partial V}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T} + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T} + \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial T\partial V}}} \right)\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right)\)
#### Note: \({\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T}\) is defined above in terms of Helmoltz derivatives. For the second derivative, \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right)\), the identity is:
\(0 = - \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right){\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T} - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {V^3}}}} \right)_T}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T} - {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)_T}\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right)\) ### (8) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial T\partial {P^2}}}} \right)\) \(\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial T\partial {P^2}}}} \right) = \left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial T\partial P}}} \right) = \frac{1}{{{{\left[ {{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)}_T}} \right]}^2}}}\left[ {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial T\partial {V^2}}}} \right) + {{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}A}}{{\partial {V^3}}}} \right)}_T}{{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)}_P}} \right]\)
#### Note: \({\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial T}}} \right)_P}\) is defined above in terms of Helmoltz derivatives. ### (9) \({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {P^3}}}} \right)_T}\)
\({\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^3}G}}{{\partial {P^3}}}} \right)_T} = {\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}V}}{{\partial {P^2}}}} \right)_T} = \frac{{{{\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)}_T}}}{{{{\left( {\frac{{{\partial ^2}A}}{{\partial {V^2}}}} \right)}_T}}}\)
#### Note: \({\left( {\frac{{\partial V}}{{\partial P}}} \right)_T}\) is defined above in terms of Helmoltz derivatives.
